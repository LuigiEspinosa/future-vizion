  • How do you define your style?

    Neo Ancient Tribal Future, the time merger between the transhuman archetype vizion of the future, entangled within the dissolution of the human soul isolated from the source of the ethereal consciousness.

  • Who are you?

    A mere specter, a collection of atoms in transient to a higher source, meanwhile encapsulated in a mammalian brain giving meaning to things that matter and also that doesn't matter. Perhaps photography helps me to see this clearly by helping me step out of this low dimensional reality.

  • What are you inspirations?

    My inspiration comes from the frustration to understand the mystery of life, reenacting emotions, persuing the intangible cryptic language of life.

    The merger between the ancient and future, gives birth to the Future Primitive.

  • How often will I get new pictures?

    A new high-resolution photograph will be delivered daily, directly to your browser every time you open a new tab.

    Yes.. any of my photos you see displayed on your tab is available for print, just contact me and let me know which one you would be interested and i will have it printed for you.

    Follow my print page, and contact me for details.

    Numerous hours went into the development and design of this Chrome Extension, it would be amazing to have your support by giving a donation.

    You welcome to contact me at ceo@metastudios.jp and also you welcome to follow me on instagram

伝記

It always boggles me how we tend to create biographies
of ourselves based on whatever is that we feel it's worth
mentioning, It's like we are trying to please and validate
our existence to other human beings.

My ego enjoys being flattered, but without the
ego, there is no creation, only the illusion of impermanence
So enough with the validation. I was named Guillermo after
a lineage of 4 other Guillermo's before me lol.
I was born in Mexico City and raised in San Francisco, CA.
My quest for the mystic arts began at an early age.

Did the whole film school thing, graphic design degree
and all that in the establishment, but what it truly allowed
me to be my true self was my fascination for ancient
and mystical places and the search for the unobtainable.

Many years I find myself living in Tokyo, crafting what I
would refer to sacred design, photography, and film
